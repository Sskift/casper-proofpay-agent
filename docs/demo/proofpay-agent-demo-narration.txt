Here is the real payment problem ProofPay is built for.

Northstar Health is about to release a forty-six thousand five hundred dollar milestone payment to Aster Cold Chain Logistics. The shipment is high-value biologics. Before anyone releases funds, the buyer needs proof that the goods arrived, stayed inside the cold-chain contract, and were submitted by the right supplier.

The supplier sends a package of off-chain evidence: invoice, bill of lading, signed delivery note, temperature export, and supplier registry snapshot. ProofPay turns that package into a structured review instead of another email thread.

First, the cockpit shows the current release state: readiness, risk, confidence, evidence coverage, and Casper proof status. This is the operator view. It does not move money by itself. It tells humans whether the payment is safe to release.

Next, the Trust Chain recomputes the case. The API accepts the evidence JSON, checks that every required document is present, and produces a decision, risk score, confidence, reasons, next actions, evidence hash, and decision hash.

The Commerce panel shows how this becomes an agent service, not just a report. The browser runs the paid proof-review route, the MCP-style settlement tool, and the no-custody settlement adapter. A duplicate invoice becomes dispute-blocked, an amount mismatch becomes finance review, and a clean shipment becomes release-ready for human approval.

In the clean case, the invoice amount matches the milestone, the shipment IDs line up, the receiver signed, the supplier registry matches, and temperature stayed inside the two to eight degree band. ProofPay recommends approve.

Then the same system handles bad cases. If the invoice amount is higher than the escrowed milestone, ProofPay does not fake confidence. It routes the case to finance review. If the invoice fingerprint has already been settled, ProofPay rejects the release and escalates fraud review.

For the fresh case used in this demo, ProofPay ran a new evidence package through the same path. The hosted API returns verified. The operator signed locally with a funded Casper Testnet key. The Casper transaction executed successfully, and the dashboard records the block height, named key, stored URef, and matching verification checks.

That is the point: the AI does the evidence work, humans keep payment authority, and Casper makes the release decision replayable for buyers, suppliers, arbiters, and judges.

ProofPay is not a generic payment gateway. It is the evidence-to-payment attestation layer missing from real-world asset settlement.
